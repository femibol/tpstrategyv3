## Your Exact Production Workflow: From Idea to Published Video

Here is the step-by-step process, optimized for someone with 5–8 hours per week producing 3 videos.

### Step 1 — Topic Research and Keyword Validation (20 minutes per video)

Use YouTube search autocomplete by typing your topic and reading the suggestions — these represent real active searches. Cross-reference with VidIQ's free plan (provides search volume and competition scores) or TubeBuddy Pro ($2.25/month, weighted keyword difficulty scores). Check Google Trends for seasonal timing. Target keywords where search volume is moderate but competition is low — the "blue ocean" for new channels.

For 2026, high-opportunity topics include:
- One Big Beautiful Bill Act tax changes
- Student loan garnishment resumption (January 2026)
- Mortgage rate forecasts
- AI-driven budgeting tools
- New charitable giving deductions

### Step 2 — Script Writing (45–60 minutes per video)

Use ChatGPT Plus ($20/month) or Claude to generate a first draft, then heavily edit with your own analysis and ERP consultant perspective.

Structure every script:
1. **Hook** (first 10 seconds — a surprising fact or bold claim)
2. **Problem identification**
3. **Explanation/Solution**
4. **Specific examples with data**
5. **Call to action**

Target **1,500–2,000 words** for a 10–12 minute video. Add pattern interrupts every 2–3 minutes (rhetorical questions, surprising stats, "here's what most people get wrong"). Mark emphasis points and pauses for the voiceover step. Your ERP background gives you a genuine edge for topics like business finance optimization, enterprise cost management, and process-driven budgeting systems.

### Step 3 — AI Voiceover Generation (15 minutes per video)

**ElevenLabs Creator plan ($22/month)** is the market leader — ultra-realistic voices with ~100 minutes of generation per month (enough for ~10 videos). Select an authoritative, calm voice. Adjust pacing and add pauses at natural break points.

For a budget alternative, ElevenLabs Starter at $5/month provides ~30 minutes. **Murf AI ($19/month)** is the runner-up, with a more polished corporate tone ideal for finance content.

> Note: PlayHT was acquired by Meta and shut down in December 2025 — remove from consideration.

### Step 4 — Visual Assembly (30–45 minutes per video)

Collect 20–30 stock footage clips from Pexels and Pixabay (both free, no attribution required). For finance-specific footage — office scenes, money visuals, charts, lifestyle shots — **Envato Elements ($16.50/month)** provides 22M+ assets including video, photos, music, and templates.

Import your voiceover into **CapCut (free)** or **DaVinci Resolve (free)**, then layer stock footage over narration, matching visuals to script content. Add text overlays for key statistics and numbers. Add auto-generated captions (CapCut does this natively). Add background music at 10–15% volume from YouTube Audio Library (free).

### Step 5 — Thumbnail Creation (10 minutes per video)

Use **Canva Pro ($13/month)** with a 1280×720 YouTube template. Without a face, your thumbnail must work harder.

Proven formulas for faceless finance:
- Bold text with 3–5 words maximum in high-contrast colors (green for money/growth, red for warnings/urgency, gold for wealth)
- Iconic financial imagery (dollar signs, credit cards, charts)
- Before/after contrast splits

Create 2–3 variants for A/B testing using YouTube's native thumbnail testing feature.

### Step 6 — Upload and SEO Optimization (15 minutes per video)

- Front-load your target keyword in the first 3–5 words of the title
- Keep titles under 60 characters
- Write a description with your primary keyword in the first sentence
- Add timestamps/chapters (each chapter title is independently searchable)
- Include affiliate links with FTC disclosure
- Add 5–15 relevant tags
- Schedule uploads for when your audience is online (check YouTube Studio → Audience tab after accumulating data)
- Use end screens in the last 20 seconds linking to a related video and a subscribe button

**Total time per video: 2.5–3.5 hours** (with practice and templates). For 3 videos per week, that's **7.5–10.5 hours** — slightly above the 5–8 hour target. To hit the lower end, batch your workflow (see the Scaling section) or use an AI video tool like InVideo AI ($25/month) to generate a rough first cut that you then polish.

## Content Strategy That Drives Views and Revenue

**The content mix that works:** Dedicate **70% of videos to evergreen content** (budgeting methods, investing basics, credit score improvement, retirement planning fundamentals) and **30% to timely/trending content** (new tax law breakdowns, market reactions, policy changes). Evergreen content builds compounding search traffic over months and years. Trending content creates subscriber spikes and algorithm momentum.

### Highest-Performing Video Formats for Faceless Finance

Ranked by views and engagement:

1. **Listicles** — "7 Money Mistakes Keeping You Broke" or "5 Best Credit Cards for 2026" consistently generate the highest CTR because they promise specific, digestible value.
2. **News commentary** — "The Housing Market Just Flipped" style videos spike in the first 48 hours and attract new subscribers rapidly.
3. **Explainers** — "How Compound Interest Actually Works" builds long-term search traffic.
4. **Comparisons** — "Roth IRA vs Traditional IRA" targets viewers actively making financial decisions, commanding premium CPMs and converting well for affiliate links.
5. **"Money mistakes" content** — taps into loss aversion psychology and generates high click-through rates.

### Optimal Video Length: 10–15 Minutes

A case study showed that a finance channel trimming 25-minute deep dives to 10–12 minutes saw retention climb from 19% to 38% with impressions nearly doubling. Videos must be at least **8 minutes** to enable mid-roll ads, which increase revenue by approximately 50%. Never pad content to hit a time target — a 12-minute video with 60% retention dramatically outperforms a 30-minute video where viewers leave after 5 minutes.

### Title Formulas That Drive Clicks in Finance

- **Number + Specific Outcome:** "3 Money Habits That Made Me $100K by 25"
- **Authority + Command:** "I'm a Financial Analyst. Do THIS Before 2026."
- **Curiosity Gap + Urgency:** "The Housing Market Just Flipped"
- **Comparison/Versus:** "High Yield Savings vs Index Funds: Where to Put $10K"
- **Warning/Mistake:** "STOP Doing This With Your Savings (It's Costing You)"

Keep titles under 60 characters. Front-load the keyword. Use digits, not spelled-out numbers. Add brackets for context: "[2026 Guide]" or "[New Rules]."

### Upload Cadence

**Upload 2–3 long-form videos per week plus 3–5 Shorts.** VidIQ's study of 5.08 million channels found that channels posting 12+ times monthly grow views **8x faster** and subscribers **3x faster** than those posting less than monthly. However, one experiment showed that jumping from 1 to 7 videos/week yielded only a 25% view increase for 7x the work — confirming that quality matters more than raw volume.

Start at 2 long-form/week for the first month while building your workflow, then scale to 3/week. Consistency on the same days (e.g., Monday, Wednesday, Friday) matters more than frequency.

### Shorts Strategy

**Shorts are a discovery engine, not a revenue engine.** YouTube Shorts hit **200 billion daily views** by mid-2025, and finance Shorts earn the highest Shorts RPM at **~$4.50 CPM** — but that translates to only $0.05–$0.30 per 1,000 views versus $10–$25 for long-form.

Use Shorts to drive subscriber growth by repurposing the most compelling 60-second segments from long-form videos. One illustrative comparison: a finance creator's 48-second Short earned $106 from 4M views, while a 12-minute long-form video earned **$45,639 from 3.9M views**. Shorts build the audience; long-form pays the bills.

## How the Algorithm Actually Treats Faceless Channels

**YouTube's recommendation system is format-agnostic — it does not penalize faceless channels.** The algorithm optimizes for engagement metrics: click-through rate, average view duration, satisfaction signals (shares, subscribes, "watch next" behavior), and session continuation.

Proof points: Kurzgesagt (20M+ subscribers), WatchMojo (25.9M), and 5-Minute Crafts (80.9M) are all faceless. A 2025 study of 300,000 viral videos confirmed that thumbnails with faces and without faces perform similarly.

The algorithm now uses **Gemini AI** to analyze video tone, on-screen elements, and semantic meaning — not just titles and tags. This means your spoken content, captions, and visual context all influence recommendations. Use named entities ("Roth IRA" instead of "this account") to help AI systems connect your content to knowledge graphs. YouTube also now actively promotes channels under 500 subscribers alongside established creators, which benefits new channels.

### Three Metrics That Determine Your Success

1. **CTR** — target 7–10%
2. **Average view duration** — target 50–60%+ retention
3. **Satisfaction signals** — post-view actions

For faceless channels specifically, retention is paramount because you lack the parasocial connection that keeps viewers watching personality-driven content. Compensate with tighter editing, pattern interrupts every 10–15 seconds, and strong opening hooks that establish value within 7 seconds.

## Legal Compliance: What You Must Do and What to Avoid Saying

### Three Non-Negotiable Legal Requirements

**1. Include disclaimers in every video.**

State verbally at the start: *"This content is for educational and informational purposes only and is not financial advice. Consult a qualified financial advisor before making decisions."* Add this text in your video description and channel About page. Use on-screen text overlay during the verbal disclaimer.

**2. Disclose all affiliate relationships per FTC rules.**

The FTC requires "clear and conspicuous" disclosure both verbally in the video and in writing near the affiliate links in your description. Use plain language: *"Some links below are affiliate links — I may earn a commission if you sign up, at no extra cost to you."* YouTube's built-in "Includes paid promotion" checkbox alone is **not sufficient**. FTC penalties reach up to **$51,744 per violation**.

**3. Maintain the education/information boundary.**

You can legally:
- Explain financial concepts
- Share general strategies
- Discuss publicly available data
- Present personal experience
- Compare products generally

You **cannot**:
- Make specific personalized investment recommendations ("You should buy X stock")
- Hold yourself out as providing individualized financial planning
- Imply a professional advisory relationship

Use phrases like "one strategy some people consider" rather than "you should do this." The SEC looks at the **substance** of your activities, not your disclaimers.

### Additional Compliance Items

- Form an LLC once revenue exceeds ~$50,000/year (costs $40–$500 depending on state) for personal asset protection and tax benefits
- All YouTube income is taxable — AdSense, sponsorships, affiliates, and digital product sales
- Make quarterly estimated tax payments once owing more than $1,000/year
- Deductible expenses include all software subscriptions, stock footage costs, freelancer payments, and home office allocation

### Copyright

- Use only properly licensed stock footage and music
- Always retain license documentation
- Government data (Federal Reserve, BLS, SEC) is generally public domain
- For third-party charts and graphs, recreate your own versions using public data rather than screenshotting
- YouTube's Content ID system will auto-scan uploads — even properly licensed music may trigger claims that require dispute with license documentation

2. **Affiliate income typically exceeds AdSense for finance channels under 100K subscribers** — a single SoFi student loan refinancing referral ($1,000) can equal a month of ad revenue. Structure every video around a natural affiliate opportunity.
